🤖 Recruitment & AI Ethics

A Discussion Activity on Responsible AI in Innovation Management

📖 Introduction

In this activity, you will explore one of the most challenging ethical dilemmas in AI-driven innovation: fairness in recruitment.

A large company has developed an AI system to shortlist job applicants. The system saves 300 hours of HR time per recruitment cycle and identifies candidates with strong potential. However, an internal audit reveals that the system consistently rejects qualified candidates from certain ethnic and socioeconomic backgrounds.

🎯 The Core Question: Should the company deploy this AI, and if so, how?

👥 The Stakeholders

Each stakeholder has a different perspective on this issue. Your group must consider all of them before making a decision.

HR Director 👔
"We save 300 hours per recruitment cycle. We cannot afford to go back to manual screening."
Diversity Officer 🌍
"This system is systematically discriminating against certain groups. It must be stopped."
AI Developer 🧠
"The algorithm is not biased — it is reflecting historical patterns in our hiring data."
Rejected Candidate 🙋
"I was qualified. How do I know the AI did not reject me unfairly?"

🧪 Your Task – Group Work

Format: Groups of 3–4 students

Step 1 – Read and Discuss

In your group, discuss the following questions:

  1. What is the benefit of using AI in this scenario?
    • Efficiency gains (300 hours saved)
    • Identifying strong candidates
    • Potential to reduce human bias
  2. What is the risk?
    • Systematic discrimination against certain groups
    • Lack of transparency
    • Legal and reputational consequences
  3. Who are the stakeholders and what do they want?
    • HR Director: Efficiency
    • Diversity Officer: Fairness
    • AI Developer: Protect their work
    • Rejected Candidate: Accountability and transparency
  4. What safeguards could be put in place?
    • Regular bias audits
    • Transparency in how the AI makes decisions
    • Human oversight of AI decisions

Step 2 – Make Your Decision

Your tribunal must decide:

Decision What It Means Your Choice (select one)
✅ Approve Deploy the AI as proposed
⚠️ Approve with Conditions Deploy the AI with specific safeguards
❌ Reject Do not use the AI for recruitment

Write a short justification for your decision. Include:

  • Why you made this choice
  • What safeguards you would put in place (if any)
  • How you would address stakeholder concerns

🏆 Bonus Activity: The Innovation Manager's Dilemma

Task: Imagine you are the Innovation Manager at the company. You know the AI has bias issues, but the HR Director is pressuring you to deploy it.

Write a 1-minute pitch to the CEO explaining:

Element What to Include Your Answer
The Problem What the AI does and why it is valuable
The Risk What the bias issue is and why it matters
The Solution What safeguards you would put in place
The Ask What you need from the CEO to proceed responsibly
"AI is a means, not an end. Be clear on what end you're trying to achieve."
– Alfredo Colas, SVP IT North America, P&G

🗣️ Class Discussion – Your Notes

After the group work, we will discuss as a class. Capture key points below:

  • "What was the most difficult part of this decision?"
  • "Should companies be allowed to use AI that is 'mostly' fair but sometimes biased?"
  • "Who should be accountable when AI causes harm?"
  • "Is it ever acceptable to reject an AI application entirely?"

🔑 Key Takeaways

Lesson Why It Matters
Efficiency is not enough AI must also be ethical and fair
Bias can be hidden AI may reflect historical patterns that are themselves biased
Transparency is essential Stakeholders must understand how decisions are made
Accountability matters Someone must be responsible for AI decisions
